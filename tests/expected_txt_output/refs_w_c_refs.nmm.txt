Referring to named items under REFERENCES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Eric Johannesson


Given a named item under REFERENCES, say with the the name
‘ITM:Euler’, what should the cross reference ‘[ITM:Euler]’ look
like? Assuming it is the first item on the list, one could either go
for

(a)   ‘REFERENCES(1)’ (or something similar), or

(b)   my preferred solution: ‘[1]’, with item labels under
      REFERENCES rendered as ‘[1]’, ‘[2]’, etc.

Having implemented (b), I can refer to (a) of References and
(b) of References below, as just indicated. I think it looks quite
nice. For good or bad, it is also familiar from the default in
LaTex.

In the future, one would perhaps like to implement som fancier
bibliography system compatible with standard bibliography tools
(e.g. zotero). But for now, I think this would suffice.
