This is a no-markup-markup document, sporting the following cool
features:

(a)   Bullet lists:

      ─     Here is one bullet item.

      ─     Here is another one.

(b)   Numbered lists with labels that are either

      (1)   automatic, or

      (ö)   custom.

      Furthermore, each item can be n̲a̲m̲e̲d̲ and r̲e̲f̲e̲r̲r̲e̲d̲ t̲o̲:

      (2)   The named item can be referred to anywhere in the
            document.

      (3)   When referring to (2) above, any common initial path
            shared between the location of the referring expression
            and the location of its referent will be omitted. The
            same applies with respect to (ä) below.

      (ä)   Of course, items with custom labels can also be named!

Due to the feature mentioned in (b)(3), a reference to (b)(2) or
(b)(ä) occurring elsewhere may appear differently.

(c)   Displayed lines of text suitable for mathematical equations,
      which are either

      (1)   automatically labeled (and named),
      (+)   custom labeled (and named), or,
            unlike (1) above, unlabeled.

Verbatim blocks on level 0 are supported:

This sentence is inside a verbatim block. Here, no escaping is
necessary: ‘\’ is produced by ‘\’, not by ‘\\’ (which in turn is
not produced by ‘\\\\’).



Of course, in verbatim blocks consecutively empty lines are
preserved, not merged into a single empty line.

Here is an ordinary text block.
